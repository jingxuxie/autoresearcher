## Current Status

The project is **active** with `last_decision: continue`, `failure_streak: 0`, and no pending checkpoint. The environment is ready in `autoresearcher_reward_to_gcrl`; GPU is visible but the accepted experiments stayed CPU/tabular.

There are now three reviewed `weak_pass` results. They provide useful early evidence for the estimator-variance premise and tabular scaling equivalence, but the research goal is **not solved**. The evidence is still limited by degenerate CliffWalking reward normalization, weak policy-disagreement evidence, and no auxiliary-goal or broader-environment validation.

## Main Findings

Confirmed findings from reviewed evidence:

- `0001` covered all 16 planned `gamma`/`r_bar` settings. Sampled means passed the stricter 3-SE review check with max z-score `2.211`.
- `0001` soft terminal variance was zero/negligible, while sampled `g_plus` counts ranged from `0.48` to `1005.02` per 10k transitions.
- `0001` tiny finite-MDP scaling equivalence passed with `max_abs_error_scaled_f_vs_q = 3.9475168023273e-08`.
- `0002` replaced the Gymnasium `CliffWalking-v0` blocker with an audited local deterministic 4x12 transition table containing 192 transition records.
- `0002` exact DP passed scaling equivalence for `gamma = 0.95, 0.99` with `max_abs_error_scaled_f_vs_q = 9.711982329463353e-10`.
- `0002` paired learning ran 20 runs across 2 gammas and 10 seeds; learned scaled `M` and normalized `Q` agreed within `5.115907697472721e-13` on sufficiently visited state-action pairs.
- `0003` reused the audited CliffWalking transition semantics and ran 30 CPU-tabular runs across `gamma = 0.95, 0.99, 0.995` and 10 seeds.
- `0003` sampled target means matched the sampled learner’s conditional expected target in all 30 runs, and sampled conditional variance exceeded the zero soft terminal-sampling variance in all 30 runs.
- `0003` soft had lower final Bellman residual in 26 of 30 runs.

Important negative results:

- In `0002` and `0003`, evaluated policies had mean raw return `-200` and success rate `0.0` under the declared normalization.
- `0003` value-error dominance was weak: soft was better in only 17 of 30 runs, and only 4 of 10 seeds at `gamma = 0.995`.

## Limitations And Risks

- The CliffWalking reward normalization maps ordinary step and goal rewards to `1` and cliff falls to `0`, making raw task success uninformative and producing policies that do not reach the goal.
- Policy-disagreement evidence is weak because many states are action-value ties; exact DP in `0002` had 37 tie states and 0 comparable non-tie states.
- `0002` paired-learning agreement is partly algebraic because both learners used identical transitions, initialization, alpha, and scaled targets.
- `0002` learned Bellman residuals were large in some runs, up to about `100`, so it does not demonstrate convergence to exact DP.
- `0003` compared sampled target means to the sampled learner’s conditional expectation, not directly to the deterministic soft learner’s recorded target; sampled-vs-soft deterministic target means exceeded tolerance in 19 of 30 runs.
- The matched transition stream in `0003` was generated by the soft learner’s behavior policy, so it is a fixed-stream estimator comparison, not an independent deployment comparison.
- No RiverSwim, FourRooms, auxiliary real-state goals, or neural/function-approximation evidence exists yet.

## Recommended Next Human Decision

Continue, but treat the current evidence as **weak early validation**, not a solved result. The next human decision should be whether to:

1. approve a narrow `0004` that fixes the degenerate reward/task-success issue with a small non-degenerate tabular setting, or
2. proceed to the charter’s next small RiverSwim long-horizon propagation test while keeping raw CliffWalking success out of the main claims.

Do not move to neural approximation, large environments, or auxiliary-goal claims until a small tabular setting shows meaningful value/error behavior without relying on degenerate policy ties.
